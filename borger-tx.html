Borger has been built on the energy industry for nearly a century. The Phillips 66 refinery complex and the surrounding oil and gas operations employ thousands of Hutchinson County residents, and many more run the small businesses, restaurants, and service companies that keep the community running. But whether you work at the plant, own a welding shop, or drive a truck for an oilfield services company, one thing is certain: your family needs dependable health insurance at a price that does not eat up your entire paycheck.

Many Borger workers find themselves in a difficult position. Employer-sponsored plans may carry deductibles of $5,000 or more, while marketplace options can be unaffordable for households that earn just above the subsidy threshold. Private PPO health plans offer an alternative — customizable coverage with lower premiums, broad networks, and the flexibility to pick a deductible that actually works for your family's finances.
